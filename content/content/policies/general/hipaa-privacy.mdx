## I. Purpose

This policy serves to establish and communicate the procedures to protect the privacy and security of patient health information (PHI) in accordance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) of 1996, the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health (HITECH) Act, and applicable state laws.

## II. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, volunteers, students, and business associates of Adapt Psychiatry who have access to protected health information in any form.

## III. Definitions

- **Protected Health Information (PHI):** Any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to an individual.
- **Electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI):** PHI that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted in electronic form.
- **Covered Entity:** Adapt Psychiatry as a healthcare provider that transmits health information electronically.
- **Business Associate:** Any person or entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of Adapt Psychiatry involving the use or disclosure of PHI.
- **Workforce:** Employees, volunteers, trainees, and other persons whose conduct is under the direct control of Adapt Psychiatry.

## IV. Privacy Rules

### Minimum Necessary Standard

Access to PHI is limited to the minimum necessary to perform job functions:

- Staff may only access PHI required for their specific role
- Do not browse patient records out of curiosity
- Report any unauthorized access immediately to the Privacy Officer

### Permitted Uses and Disclosures

PHI may be used or disclosed without patient authorization for:

1. **Treatment** - Providing, coordinating, or managing healthcare
2. **Payment** - Obtaining reimbursement for healthcare services
3. **Healthcare Operations** - Quality assessment, training, business planning

### Patient Rights

Patients have the right to:

| Right | Description | Timeframe |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Access | Inspect and obtain a copy of their PHI | 30 days |
| Amendment | Request corrections to their records | 60 days |
| Accounting | Receive a list of disclosures | 60 days |
| Restrictions | Request limits on uses/disclosures | N/A |
| Confidential Communications | Request alternative contact methods | Reasonable |
| Complaint | File a complaint about privacy practices | N/A |

## V. Security Safeguards

### Administrative Safeguards

1. **Security Management Process**
   - Risk analysis conducted annually
   - Risk management procedures implemented
   - Sanction policy for violations

2. **Assigned Security Responsibilities**
   - Security Officer: [Name]
   - Privacy Officer: [Name]

3. **Workforce Security**
   - Authorization procedures for access
   - Clearance procedures for new hires
   - Termination procedures

4. **Training**
   - Initial HIPAA training within 30 days of hire
   - Annual refresher training
   - Additional training when policies change

### Physical Safeguards

1. **Facility Access Controls**
   - Controlled access to buildings
   - Visitor sign-in procedures
   - Workstation security

2. **Workstation Use**
   - Screens positioned away from public view
   - Automatic screen locks after 5 minutes
   - No PHI on personal devices without approval

3. **Device and Media Controls**
   - Proper disposal of PHI (shredding, secure deletion)
   - Media re-use procedures
   - Accountability for hardware

### Technical Safeguards

1. **Access Control**
   - Unique user IDs for all systems
   - Role-based access controls
   - Automatic logoff after inactivity

2. **Audit Controls**
   - Activity logging on all systems containing ePHI
   - Regular audit log review
   - Suspicious activity investigation

3. **Integrity Controls**
   - Mechanisms to authenticate ePHI
   - Protection against improper alteration/destruction

4. **Transmission Security**
   - Encryption for ePHI transmission
   - Secure email protocols
   - VPN for remote access

## VI. Breach Notification

### Definition

A breach is an impermissible use or disclosure under the Privacy Rule that compromises the security or privacy of PHI.

### Breach Response Process

1. **Immediate Action (Within 1 Hour)**
   - Contain the breach
   - Prevent further unauthorized access
   - Notify Privacy Officer

2. **Investigation (Within 24 Hours)**
   - Determine scope of breach
   - Identify affected individuals
   - Assess risk of harm

3. **Notification Requirements**
   - **Affected Individuals:** Within 60 days
   - **HHS:** If 500+ individuals affected, immediately; otherwise annually
   - **Media:** If 500+ individuals in a state/jurisdiction

## VII. Business Associates

### Requirements

- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) required before sharing PHI
- BAAs must specify permitted uses and safeguards
- Business associates must report breaches to Adapt Psychiatry

### Approved Business Associates

- Electronic Health Record vendors
- Billing services
- Cloud storage providers
- Telehealth platforms

## VIII. Training Requirements

### Initial Training

All workforce members must complete HIPAA training within 30 days of hire, covering:

- HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules
- Adapt Psychiatry policies and procedures
- Patient rights
- Breach notification procedures

### Annual Training

Refresher training required annually, including:

- Policy updates
- Recent breach examples
- Security best practices

### Documentation

Training completion must be documented and maintained for 6 years.
