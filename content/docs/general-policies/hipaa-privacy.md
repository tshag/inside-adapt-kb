# HIPAA Privacy and Security Policy

## I. Purpose

This policy serves to establish and communicate the procedures to protect the privacy and security of patient health information in accordance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) of 1996.

## II. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, volunteers, and business associates of Adapt Psychiatry who have access to protected health information (PHI).

## III. Definitions

- **PHI (Protected Health Information):** Any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to an individual.
- **Covered Entity:** Adapt Psychiatry as a healthcare provider that transmits health information electronically.
- **Business Associate:** Any person or entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of Adapt Psychiatry involving the use or disclosure of PHI.

## IV. Privacy Rules

### Minimum Necessary Standard

Access to PHI is limited to the minimum necessary to perform job functions:

1. Staff may only access PHI required for their specific role
2. Do not browse patient records out of curiosity
3. Report any unauthorized access immediately

### Patient Rights

Patients have the right to:
- Access their medical records
- Request amendments to their records
- Receive an accounting of disclosures
- Request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures
- File a complaint about privacy practices

## V. Security Safeguards

### Administrative Safeguards

- Security awareness training for all staff
- Assigned security responsibilities
- Information access management
- Workforce training and management

### Physical Safeguards

- Secure workstations and devices
- Controlled facility access
- Proper disposal of PHI
- Workstation use and security policies

### Technical Safeguards

- Unique user IDs and passwords
- Automatic logoff after inactivity
- Encryption of data in transit and at rest
- Audit controls and activity monitoring

## VI. Breach Notification

In the event of a suspected or confirmed breach:

1. **Immediate Action:** Contain the breach and prevent further unauthorized access
2. **Report:** Notify the Privacy Officer within 1 hour
3. **Investigation:** Cooperate fully with the breach investigation
4. **Documentation:** Document all relevant details

## VII. Training Requirements

All staff must complete:
- Initial HIPAA training within 30 days of hire
- Annual refresher training
- Additional training when policies change

- Criminal penalties under federal law
- Civil monetary penalties
- Loss of professional licensure
