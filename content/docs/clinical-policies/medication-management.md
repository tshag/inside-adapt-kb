# Medication Management Protocol

## Overview

This protocol outlines the standard procedures for medication management at Adapt Psychiatry.

## Initial Assessment

### Before Prescribing

1. Complete comprehensive psychiatric evaluation
2. Review medical history and current medications
3. Assess for substance use
4. Evaluate for contraindications
5. Document informed consent discussion

### Required Documentation

- Diagnosis with supporting criteria
- Target symptoms for medication
- Previous medication trials and responses
- Patient preferences and concerns
- Risk assessment

## Follow-Up Schedule

| Medication Type | Initial Follow-Up | Ongoing Monitoring |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| New antidepressant | 2-4 weeks | Every 3 months |
| Mood stabilizer | 1-2 weeks | Monthly for 3 months, then every 3 months |
| Stimulant | 1 week | Monthly |
| Antipsychotic | 1-2 weeks | Every 3 months |

## Side Effect Monitoring

### Common Side Effects to Assess

- **Antidepressants:** GI upset, sleep changes, sexual side effects
- **Mood Stabilizers:** Tremor, weight gain, cognitive effects
- **Stimulants:** Appetite suppression, sleep disturbance, anxiety
- **Antipsychotics:** Weight gain, metabolic changes, movement disorders

### Documentation Requirements

Document at each visit:
- Medication adherence
- Side effects present
- Efficacy assessment
- Any dose adjustments

## Refill Policies

1. Controlled substances require appointment within 30 days
2. Non-controlled medications may have 90-day supplies
3. Early refill requests require clinical review
4. Lost medication policies apply

## Emergency Protocols

### Suicidal Ideation

1. Assess risk level
2. Consider hospitalization if high risk
3. Document safety plan
4. Increase follow-up frequency

### Adverse Reactions

1. Discontinue medication if severe
2. Provide appropriate treatment
3. Document and report
4. Follow up within 24-48 hours
